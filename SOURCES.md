# Sources & References

## Ancient & Classical Sources

### Greek & Roman
- Herodotus. (c. 450 BCE). *Histories*, Book III-IV. Scythian griffins guarding gold, Arimaspi conflicts.
- Aeschylus. (5th century BCE). *Prometheus Bound*. Griffin references.
- Ctesias. (c. 400 BCE). *Indica*. Griffins in India.
- Pliny the Elder. (77 CE). *Natural History*, Book VII, X. Griffin descriptions.
- Pausanias. (c. 150 CE). *Description of Greece*. Griffins in Greek art and temples.
- Aelian. (c. 200 CE). *On the Nature of Animals*, Book IV. Griffin characteristics.

### Ancient Near Eastern
- Archaeological artifacts from Mesopotamia, Egypt, Persia (3000+ BCE)
- Cylinder seals and decorative art
- Palace reliefs and sculptures

## Medieval Sources

### Bestiaries
- *Physiologus* (c. 2nd-4th century CE). Early Christian bestiary.
- *Aberdeen Bestiary* (c. 12th century). Illuminated bestiary with griffin entry.
- *Bodleian Library MS. Ashmole 1511* (13th century). English bestiary.
- Isidore of Seville. (c. 600 CE). *Etymologiae*. Encyclopedia with griffin description.

### Medieval Literature
- Dante Alighieri. (c. 1320). *The Divine Comedy*, Purgatorio. Griffin allegory for Christ.
- *The Travels of Sir John Mandeville* (14th century). Griffin accounts.
